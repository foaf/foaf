Welcome to RDFWeb

RDFWeb is an experimental linked information system.

RDFWeb explores some interconnected applications of the semantic web. It features a distributed RDF-indexed photo archive, the friend-of-a-friend information linking system, and various other fun things.

RDFWeb.org revamp, finally

09 February 2002: The RDFWeb site has finally been updated. The new site remains loyal to the old vapourware theme, promising more than it delivers, lurching from incomplete high level overviews to obscure technical detail without providing much in the middle. The tentative sitemap draws together some previously scattered pointers to RDFWeb-related documents, and highlights a new focus on the ambitiously silly FOAF co-depiction experiment. Will a co-depiction craze sweep the Web? Only after someone writes the HOWTO... (news archive)

WWW2002 paper rejected

26 January 2002: Plans for flaunting RDFWeb vapourware at the WWW2002 Web conference suffered a minor setback this week, when peer reviewers rejected as 'brain-dumpy' a paper describing some RDFWeb-inspired organisational modelling work at ILRT. We'll probably be there anyway due to other commitments so who cares... :) (news archive)

RDFWeb site redesign

26 January 2002: At long last, the RDFWeb site redesign has been postponed again. The new design fails to replace the crufty scribbles currently served by rdfweb.org. Failing to exploit CSS1, XSLT and other new fangled technologies in any interesting way, the undeployed redesign does at least have fewer words on the home page. (news archive)
